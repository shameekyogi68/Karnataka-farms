About Monstera Deliciosa

Native to the tropical forests of Southern Mexico and Panama, the Monstera Deliciosa has become a global icon of modern interior design. Its unique fenestrations (leaf holes) develop as the plant matures, creating an ever-changing living sculpture.

At Karnataka Farms, all our grafts and seedlings are nurtured in our Byndoor research facility. Each plant is meticulously hardened off to ensure it thrives in Indian coastal and inland agricultural conditions, promising maximum yield and disease resistance.

Care Instructions

  • Light: Place in bright, indirect sunlight. An east-facing window is ideal.
  • Water: Water thoroughly when the top 1-2 inches of soil feel dry. Reduce in winter.
  • Humidity: Prefers 60%+ humidity. Mist occasionally or use a pebble tray.
  • Fertilizer: Feed with balanced liquid fertilizer every 2 weeks during growing season (March-September).
  • Pruning: Remove yellowing leaves at the base to redirect energy to new growth.

Plant Care Guide

Expert tips straight from Benny Chacko and our nursery staff to keep your exotic fruits and ornamentals thriving.

Watering Exotic Fruits

Tropical and exotic fruit trees like Jackfruit and Mango require deep, infrequent watering. Allow the top 2-3 inches of soil to dry out completely before watering thoroughly. Never let the roots sit in waterlogged soil.

Fertilization Schedule

Apply a balanced organic fertilizer every 2-3 months during the growing season. For fruiting trees, switch to a high-potassium mix just before flowering to encourage heavy fruit set.

Acclimatization

When you receive a new plant from our Byndoor nursery, place it in a shaded area for 3-5 days to let it recover from shipping shock before moving it to its final sunny spot.
